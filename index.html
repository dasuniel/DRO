How to use this

read me first · 2 min

30–60 minutes a day beats 6 hours on Sunday. A “week” here means roughly 5 practice sessions — if it takes you 10 days, that’s fine. Don’t skip the fundamentals to get to manga faster; weeks 9–12 only work because of weeks 1–8.

Each session: do the warm-up below (5 min), then work through the current week’s drills. Check a drill off to ink it — blue pencil is rough work, ink is done. Checking anything keeps your streak stamp alive.

Draw everything in Procreate. Keep this page open in Split View next to it. Mistakes are the actual product of practice — never erase a gesture drawing, just restate the line.

Rough rule: if a drill felt easy, you can move on. If it felt impossible, do half of it today and half tomorrow. “Slightly hard” is the zone where you improve.
☀

Daily warm-up

5 min · every session, forever
  • 10 straight lines: place two dots far apart, connect each pair with one confident stroke.
  • 10 circles — 5 clockwise, 5 counter-clockwise.
  • 5 ellipses in a row, all the same tilt and roundness.
  • 3 boxes floating in random rotations.
  • 1 gesture pose, 30 seconds (use the Pose timer tab).
Draw warm-ups from the shoulder, big and fast, on a throwaway canvas. They are not supposed to be pretty.
Procreate setup · read once
You work only in Procreate, so the how-to for every exercise now lives on the drill itself. This is the one-time setup — learn these gestures, tools and brushes in week 1, then forget you read it.
1

Canvas setup

do this once

Make one canvas called DAILY at Screen Size and reuse it for warm-ups and drills — zero friction to start. For pieces you want to finish properly, create 3000 × 3000 px or A4 · 300 DPI.

Bigger canvases give you fewer maximum layers — that’s normal, not a bug. For line practice you only ever need 1–2 layers anyway.
2

Gestures you must know

muscle memory · week 1
  • 2-finger tapUndo (hold to undo fast)
  • 3-finger tapRedo
  • 4-finger tapHide / show the interface
  • 3-finger swipe downCut / copy / paste menu
  • PinchZoom & rotate; quick pinch snaps back to fit
  • Touch & hold canvasEyedropper — pick any color on screen
  • Drag the color dotColorDrop: drop it inside a closed shape to fill
  • Touch & hold eraserErase with your current brush (clean matching edges)
  • Slide the sidebarBrush size (top) and opacity (bottom)
3

Tools that actually matter

everything else can wait
  • QuickShapeDraw a line/circle and hold the pencil down at the end — it snaps clean. Tap “Edit Shape” to adjust. Great for panels; banned during line drills.
  • StreamLinePer-brush smoothing (tap brush → Stroke Path). Keep it low for practice so you see your real line; crank it high when inking manga.
  • Alpha Lock2-finger swipe right on a layer — you can now only paint on existing pixels. Shading inside a silhouette becomes trivial.
  • Clipping MaskTap a layer → Clipping Mask: it only shows where the layer below has pixels. The standard way to shade flat colors.
  • Drawing GuideWrench → Canvas → Drawing Guide → Edit. Symmetry for faces, Perspective for backgrounds (place your vanishing points, toggle Assisted Drawing per layer).
  • Reference windowWrench → Canvas → Reference — float a photo or pose next to your drawing instead of switching apps.
  • Flip horizontalWrench → Canvas → Flip. Do it constantly — wonky proportions jump out instantly in mirror view.
  • Select + TransformLasso a too-big eye or short arm and nudge it. Fixing beats redrawing.
4

Brushes — defaults are enough

don’t buy packs yet
  • Sketching6B Pencil or Narinder Pencil — your daily drivers for weeks 1–8.
  • InkingStudio Pen (crisp, pressure-sensitive line weight) and Syrup (juicier). Manga lives here.
  • ShadingSoft Brush (Airbrushing) for gradients — use sparingly; pencils keep more life.
  • PaintingRound Brush when you start coloring.
  • ScreentonesExplore the Textures set, or import free halftone brushes when you reach week 11.
A new brush never fixes a fundamentals problem. If a drawing fails, it’s almost always construction or values — not the pen.
5

The manga layer stack

top → bottom · from week 9
  • Effects / tones — speed lines, screentone, sparkles
  • Highlights — small layer, Add or Screen blend mode
  • Shadows — one gray on a Multiply layer, clipped to the flats
  • Flat colors — base colors, filled with ColorDrop
  • INK — clean lines, Studio Pen, StreamLine high
  • Blue rough — your sketch in light blue, opacity ~35%
  • Background
Sketch rough in blue, ink on a layer above, then drop the rough’s opacity — the exact workflow this app’s blue→ink design is stolen from. Group layers per character.
6

Saving & seeing progress

future-you says thanks

Wrench → Share: PNG to post, PSD as a layered backup of finished pieces. Export the time-lapse of anything you finish — watching yourself draw is the fastest feedback there is.

Once a month, redraw the very first thing you ever drew in week 1. Keep them all in one Procreate stack called PROGRESS. This folder will become your favorite thing on the iPad.
